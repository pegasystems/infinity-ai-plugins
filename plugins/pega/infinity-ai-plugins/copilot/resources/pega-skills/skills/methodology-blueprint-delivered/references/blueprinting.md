## Purpose

The first phase of Blueprint Delivered. Transforms initial ideas and requirements into a
**high-fidelity Blueprint** -- a dynamic, visual model that replaces static documentation
and establishes the single source of truth for delivery.

Blueprinting activates the "golden thread" by redefining design and bringing what would
typically be Sprint Zero requirements into a structured, AI-powered process.

## Key Differentiators

- **Speed and Efficiency** -- AI compresses timelines from weeks/months to days
- **Unified, Early Alignment** -- All stakeholders engaged from the start
- **Living Blueprint** -- Dynamic, import-ready artifact (not a static document)
- **Process-first Clarity** -- Visualizes user experience without wireframes
- **Embedded Validation** -- Reduces rework through early, continuous validation

## Stage 1: Discover -- Align and Capture

### Goal
Establish groundwork by aligning business and IT stakeholders on objectives, capturing
requirements, and building a solid foundation.

### Key Activities

1. **Kick-off Session** -- Establish operational objectives, secure resources, outline
   discovery strategy
2. **Application Discovery** -- End-to-end process walkthroughs, structured interviews,
   ecosystem mapping
3. **AI-powered Analysis** -- Generative AI ingests SOPs, legacy documentation, video
   recordings to extract insights, surface requirements, and identify gaps
4. **Completeness Validation** -- Confirm enough content exists for the Design stage

### Best Practices
- Initiate with a collaborative kickoff
- Accelerate analysis with AI tools
- Map the "as-is" state before designing the "to-be"
- Engage stakeholders proactively
- Adjust discovery depth to project scope
- Define clear success criteria upfront

### Deliverables
- Clearly defined objectives and scope
- Validated artifacts usable for solution design

## Stage 2: Design -- Validate the Blueprint

### Goal
Transform discovery insights into a high-fidelity Blueprint that guides implementation
with precision and confidence.

### Key Activities

1. **Blueprint Orientation** -- Team learns Blueprint platform capabilities; shared
   understanding of what "high-fidelity" means
2. **Blueprint Generation** -- AI synthesizes discovery materials into draft Case Types,
   workflows, and Data Models
3. **Workflow Detailing** -- Define every stage, alternate path, and persona
4. **Collaborative Validation** -- Business and IT stakeholders review, refine, and
   enrich the AI-generated Blueprint
5. **Blueprint Enrichment** -- Add detailed Data Models, Personas, business rules,
   integration specifications
6. **High-Fidelity Check** -- Formal validation that the Blueprint is complete, coherent,
   and import-ready
7. **Scope and Budget Finalization** -- Accurate project estimates and team composition
8. **Stakeholder Sign-off** -- Formal approval of the solution design

### Best Practices
- Centralize documentation in the Blueprint
- Facilitate collaborative workshops with short feedback loops
- Design for reuse across case types
- Use checkpoint-based sign-off procedures

### Deliverables
- Validated and versioned high-fidelity Blueprint
- Validated process workflows, Data Models, integrations, and Personas
- Formal stakeholder sign-off on solution design, scope, and budget

## Stage 3: Prepare -- Confirm and Accelerate

### Goal
Final checkpoint before the build. Ensures every aspect is validated, aligned, and ready
for seamless implementation.

### Readiness Checks

| Area | What is validated |
|------|-------------------|
| **Blueprint Readiness** | Design validated, versioned, and import-ready |
| **Project Readiness** | Technical environments, connectivity, integrations confirmed |
| **Team and Governance** | Roles, responsibilities, training, governance structures defined |

### Key Activities

1. **Version the Blueprint** -- Secure current work, make it import-ready and
   referenceable as a baseline
2. **Environment Preparation** -- Set up technical environments, test connectivity
3. **Integration Confirmation** -- Validate all external integration points
4. **Team Training** -- Ensure all team members are trained on tools and processes
5. **Governance Setup** -- Establish communication protocols and governance structures

### Best Practices
- Conduct formal readiness reviews
- Clearly define individual responsibilities
- Use the Blueprint for accurate estimation
- Pre-build validate all technical components

### Deliverables
- Validated and operational technical environments and integrations
- Fully trained project team
- Accurate project estimates and optimized team composition
- Documented governance structure and communication plan
- Clear implementation transition plan with handoff protocols
